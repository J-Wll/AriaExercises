Reasons for changes

The original buttons are made of a modified span tag within HTML, this is bad practice as it requires defining additional attributes that are inherently present in the native, semantic tag of button. These attributes, if configured incorrectly would lead to accessibility problems for some users.

Changing the span tag to a button and removing the now unnecessary attributes not only makes the HTML more clear but makes sure that the best practice and widely used roles, states and attributes are used.

Another change was in the colouring of the suggested replacement button, slightly lightening the background and darkening the text. This slight change in colours maintains the original design ethos yet is enough to enhance the contrast ratio past the specified AA and AAA contrast standards.

While this slight change may not be picked up by many users, it might make a significant difference for those less-abled in terms of vision. By improving the experience of these users on our website, we increase their likelyhood of purchasing our products and appreciating our brand.
